Female Infertility

Female infertility is caused by poor ovulation, damaged fallopian tubes or problems with the womb. After infertility testing, women can be treated with medications, surgery, IUI or IVF.

  • Clomid Pills — prescribed to improve egg ovulation, takes 2-3 months to conceive
  • Damaged Fallopian Tubes — tubal infertility is best treated with IVF
  • Ectopic Pregnancy — unruptured ectopic is often treated with medication
  • Endometriosis — more than 50% women with minimal to mild endometriosis have a baby with IVF

Miscarriages (Loss of Pregnancy)

Miscarriages and pregnancy losses at different stages of pregnancy are on the increase. Most Ob/Gyn doctors have no time and no experience to treat patients who lose every pregnancy they conceive.

Intrauterine Inseminations

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI) is a medical procedure to introduce sperm separated from seminal fluid into women uterus. Minimum 5-7 million moving sperm is needed.

Pregnancy Rates: Chances for each IUI treatment to produce pregnancy is 15%.

IUI is most effective for couples with minor sperm deficiencies, erectile dysfunction and for women using frozen donor sperm.
